# DESIGN_INTENT

Scope: Homepage only (Phase 1)  
Mode: Design-First, Token-First  
Reference interpretation: Framer vibe inspired (atmosphere, not clone)

## Desired vibe (12 bullets)

- Calm first impression with visual breathing room.
- Premium without looking corporate-cold.
- Airy structure with clear blocks and pacing.
- Confident hierarchy: headline, subcopy, then actions.
- Soft depth through layered surfaces and subtle shadows.
- Friendly but disciplined UI language.
- Human-centered imagery that supports trust.
- Reduced noise: fewer visual collisions, fewer competing accents.
- Consistent rhythm between hero and following sections.
- Buttons and cards feel intentional, not generic defaults.
- Navigation feels native to the hero, not bolted on.
- Overall tone supports safety, clarity, and long-term trust.

## Do rules (8)

- Use generous section spacing and consistent vertical rhythm.
- Keep hero as dominant mood-setter in the first viewport.
- Maintain clear text contrast with soft, non-harsh surfaces.
- Use one primary brand accent and one support accent only.
- Keep heading scales clearly separated from body and meta text.
- Use rounded corners and soft shadow depth consistently.
- Keep button hierarchy obvious (primary vs secondary).
- Preserve EN/ES behavior with identical structure and styling.

## Don't rules (8)

- Do not use harsh black/white contrast blocks as default surfaces.
- Do not crowd sections with tight spacing or dense stacks.
- Do not mix many accent colors or high-saturation gradients.
- Do not let nav/header appear detached from hero composition.
- Do not overuse borders/shadows that create visual clutter.
- Do not use meme-chaos visual language or noisy ornaments.
- Do not regress into old layout skeleton with new paint only.
- Do not introduce content or feature scope beyond homepage styling/layout.

## What success looks like on the homepage

Success means the homepage immediately feels calm, premium, and coherent: the hero sets tone and hierarchy in one glance, the header/navigation reads as part of that first composition, section spacing feels intentional and breathable, and the full page feels like one designed system rather than mixed legacy fragments. The result should look modern and trustworthy while preserving current content and bilingual behavior.
